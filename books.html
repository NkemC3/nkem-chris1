I. What it's All About

Whence come the thousands of photographs used every month by newspaper and magazines?

More than that, whence do the photographs come which are used by the makers of calendars, postcards, for advertisement, and for illustrating books, stories and articles?

This little book is a practical, up-to-the-minute answer to the question: "How can i make my camera-work profitable ?"

A. H. Beardsley,
Publisher, Photo-Era Magazine.

The camerist does not have to get in air-ship and fly to Africa in order to produce photographs that will sell. Read what Waldon Fawcett says, himself a success at selling his photographs:

The photographer is apt to think that all his ambitions would be realised if only he could journey to foreign shores or to distant corners of our country; or if he could attend the spectacular events that focus the attention of the world now and then. This is a delusion. The real triumph is that of the photographer who utilizes the material ready at hand in its own district, be it large or small.

The markets may be classified briefly:

  1. Newspaper
  2. magazines
  3. Postcard-makers
  4. Calendar-makers
  5. Art-study producers
  6. Illustrations for books
  7. Illustrations for articles
  8. Prints for advertising
